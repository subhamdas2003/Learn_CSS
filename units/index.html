UNITS IN CSS

There are two types of units in CSS: absolute and relative.

Absolute units

Absolute units are fixed and do not change based on the user's settings or preferences. They are typically used for print styles or when you want to ensure a specific size regardless of the device.

examples include:

  • px (pixels) (16px=100%)
  • cm (centimeters)
  • mm (millimeters)
  • in (inches)
  • pt (points)
  • pc (picas)

Note: 1 inch = 2.54 cm, 1 cm = 10 mm, 1 pt = 1/72 inch, and 1 pc = 12 pt.

Relative Units

Relative units are flexible and adapt to the user's settings or preferences. They are commonly used for responsive designs, allowing elements to scale based on the viewport size or user preferences.

Example Includes:

  • em (relative to the font-size of the element)
  • rem (relative to the font-size of the root element)
  • vw (viewport width, 1vw = 1% of the viewport width)
  • vh (viewport height, 1vh = 1% of the viewport height)
  • vmin (minimum of viewport width or height)
  • vmax (maximum of viewport width or height)
